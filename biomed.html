Biology & Medicine



An Introduction to Careers in Biology and Medicine

A college education pursuing Biomedical Science involves majoring in a breadth of various STEM subjects such as...

  • Human Anatomy & Physiology
  • Genetics
  • Biochemistry - the study of chemical processes within and relating to living organisms.
  • Microbiology - the study of microscopic organisms, such as bacteria, viruses, archaea, fungi and protozoa.
  • Nutrition
  • Kinesiology - the study of the mechanics of human movement and how they impact our health and wellbeing.
  • Epidemiology - the study of the distribution of disease. (ex. COVID-19)

Exploring Careers in Biology and Medicine

  • Biomedical Engineer - Design equipment and devices, such as artificial internal organs, replacements for body parts, and machines for diagnosing medical problems.


  • Forensic Scientist - Forensic science technicians work with law enforcement to analyze evidence and investigate crimes. The evidence gathered from crime scenes may include fingerprints, blood, hair, bullet fragments, photographs, and more.


  • Physician - Doctors are also called physicians and they can be either medical doctors (M.D.) or doctors of osteopathic medicine (D.O.). Physicians examine patients; take medical histories; prescribe medications; and order, perform, and interpret diagnostic tests. They often counsel patients on diet, hygiene, and preventive healthcare.

Mary Grace Salayog

Clinical Laboratory Scientist at St. Rose Hospital in Oakland, California

Q: Who is she?

Mary Grace Salayog is originally from the Philippines and had graduated college there as well. She moved to California in 2015 and climbed her way up the ranks as a Lead CLS in the Blood Bank. Despite the challenges of being a minority at the hospital and also the youngest and least experienced, Grace had been further promoted, first as a General Supervisor and then currently, a Lab Manager at a sister hospital.

Q: How did she leave her impact in the field?

"As a Clinical Laboratory Scientist (CLS), I perform high complexity laboratory testing (ie. Blood Bank, Chemistry, Coagulation, Hematology, Serology and Clinical Microscopy). These tests indicate complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, covid testing etc. electronically through our lab information system which is then sent over to physicians. They then proceed to interpret the tests in order to come up with a reasonable diagnosis for their patients."


Quyen Nguyen, M.D., Ph.D

Professor at the Department of Surgery University of California San Diego

Q: Who is she?

Quyen T. Nguyen, MD, PhD, is a board-certified head and neck surgeon who specializes in neurotology, or the management of diseases of the inner ear, temporal bone, and skull base, including tumors and other conditions. Her main focus is in cochlear implants for hearing restoration and facial nerve surgery.

Q: How did she leave her impact on the field?

In collaboration with Dr. Roger Tsien - who had won a Nobel Prize for Chemistry in 2008 - Quyen is known for her contribution in the development of fluorescence-guided surgery (FGS) with live molecular navigation, also known as "color-coded surgery". Quyen's idea of fluorescent guided surgery through injectable probes allows operating surgeons to get a more accurate scale of how serious a tumor is. Since the probe causes the tumor to light up, surgeons will be able to develop more precise methods to take out a tumor therefore increasing survival rates in patients and their quality of life.
